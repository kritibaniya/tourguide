Food is not just only a basic physiological need. It can be lifestyle, culture, tradition and much more. Eating habit in Nepal is pretty different than in the west. For example, usually people have more than two warm meals a day and it is not common to use cutlery to eat. Nepali dishes are often filled with varieties of spices and herbs. A great combination of those spices makes the food really tasty. This article will give you an insight of what Nepali people eat in a day.

Chya

Nepal wakes up pretty early. People gets up usually between 5 and 6 am and sip their morning tea. Tea is called chya in Nepali and is more than just black tea with milk. People add various spices like cardamom, cinnamon, and ginger. It clearly makes the tea much more flavorful.

Dal Bhat

This is the most common dish from Nepal. People usually eat it for both lunch around 10am and dinner around 7pm. Daal means lentil soup and bhat means rice. Dal bhat is served with different types of vegetables, curry, and pickles for variation.

Momo

You will be seeing these dumplings in every restaurant menu. These dumplings can have different types of filling, but the spices will give all of them same great taste. Momo comes with chutney which you are supposed to dip your momo and eat. Ps: It can be very hot.

Sel roti

These donut looking things are called Sel-roti. They are made out of rice flour, sugar, butter and deep fried in oil. They are a little bit sweet but is served with savory side dishes. A fusion of sweet and savory can be mind-blowing.

Juju-dhau

Sweet yogurt made in a special kind of pot is a famous dessert from Nepal. It is also known as “king of yogurt”. You must try it to understand reason behind the hype.
